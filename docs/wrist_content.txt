=== WRIST REGION ===
CARPAL TUNNEL SYNDROME (CTS)
[List Paragraph] Age
[List Paragraph] 30–60 years (Common CTS group)
[List Paragraph] <30 years (Consider overuse or other neuropathy)
[List Paragraph] Where is the pain located ?
[List Paragraph] Palmar wrist/hand (Median nerve distribution → CTS)
[List Paragraph] Dorsal wrist (Suggests other pathology)
[List Paragraph] Symptoms distribution
[List Paragraph] Thumb, index, middle, and radial half of ring finger (CLASSIC CTS)
[List Paragraph] Whole hand (Consider proximal nerve or systemic issue)
[List Paragraph] How did the symptoms begin ?
[List Paragraph] Gradual (Typical CTS)
[List Paragraph] Sudden (Consider trauma)
[List Paragraph] What is the pain type?
[List Paragraph] Tingling, numbness, burning (Neuropathic → CTS)
[List Paragraph] Sharp localized pain (Musculoskeletal)
[List Paragraph] Do you experience night symptoms?
[List Paragraph] Yes (VERY STRONG CTS INDICATOR)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Do symptoms wake you from sleep?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Classic CTS)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Do you shake your hand to relieve symptoms?
[List Paragraph] Yes (“Flick sign” → HIGHLY SPECIFIC CTS)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] What aggravates the pain
[List Paragraph] Repetitive wrist use (Typing, gripping) (CTS)
[List Paragraph] Impact/trauma (Consider injury)
[List Paragraph] Is there weakness (grip or thumb)?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Thenar involvement → advanced CTS)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Does pain radiate above the wrist?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Possible proximal nerve involvement)
[List Paragraph] No (Supports CTS)
Objective / Observation
[List Paragraph] Is there thenar muscle wasting?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Chronic CTS)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there reduced sensation in median nerve distribution?
[List Paragraph] Yes (CTS)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there weak thumb opposition?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Median nerve motor deficit)
[List Paragraph] No
Red Flag
[List Paragraph] Severe progressive weakness?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Urgent referral)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Symptoms involving entire arm?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Cervical pathology)
[List Paragraph] No
SPECIAL CONFIRMATORY TESTS
[List Paragraph] Phalen’s Test
[List Paragraph] Patient flexes wrists fully and holds position.
[List Paragraph] Sign is positive if numbness/tingling occurs.
[List Paragraph] If positive, indicates CTS.
[List Paragraph] Tinel’s Sign
[List Paragraph] Examiner taps over carpal tunnel.
[List Paragraph] Sign is positive if tingling in median nerve distribution.
[List Paragraph] If positive, indicates CTS.
[List Paragraph] Carpal Compression Test
[List Paragraph] Examiner applies direct pressure over carpal tunnel.
[List Paragraph] Sign is positive if symptoms are reproduced.
[List Paragraph] If positive, indicates CTS.
DE QUERVAIN’S TENOSYNOVITIS
[List Paragraph] Age
[List Paragraph] 30–50 years (Common group)
[List Paragraph] Any age (With repetitive use)
[List Paragraph] Where is the pain located?
[List Paragraph] Radial wrist (Thumb side) (KEY SIGN)
[List Paragraph] Ulnar wrist (Other pathology)
[List Paragraph] How did the pain begin?
[List Paragraph] Gradual (Overuse)
[List Paragraph] Sudden (Less common)
[List Paragraph] What aggravates the pain?
[List Paragraph] Thumb movement/gripping (CLASSIC)
[List Paragraph] Wrist extension (Less specific)
[List Paragraph] Is there pain with lifting objects (e.g., child, kettle)?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Very typical)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there swelling near thumb base?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Inflammation of tendon sheath)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Do you feel creaking sensation?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Tenosynovitis)
[List Paragraph] No
Objective / Observation
[List Paragraph] Is there ttenderness over radial styloid?
[List Paragraph] Yes (PRIMARY FINDING)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there pain with resisted thumb extension/abduction?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Abductor Pollicis Longus (APL) and Extensor Pollicis Brevis (EPB) involvement)
[List Paragraph] No
Red Flag
History of trauma?
a. Yes (Rule out fracture)
b. No
SPECIAL CONFIRMATORY TESTS
[List Paragraph] Finkelstein’s Test
[List Paragraph] Patient makes a fist with thumb inside fingers. Examiner ulnar deviates wrist.
[List Paragraph] Sign is positive if sharp pain at radial wrist.
[List Paragraph] If positive, indicates De Quervain’s.
[List Paragraph] WHAT Test (Wrist Hyperflexion Abduction of Thumb)
[List Paragraph] Patient hyperflexes wrist and abducts thumb against resistance.
[List Paragraph] Sign is positive if pain is reproduced.
[List Paragraph] If positive, indicates De Quervain’s.
WRIST SPRAIN (LIGAMENTOUS INJURY)
[List Paragraph] Age
[List Paragraph] Where is the pain located ?
[List Paragraph] Localized to wrist (Depends on ligament)
[List Paragraph] Diffuse (Less specific)
[List Paragraph] Is there any history of trauma?
[List Paragraph] Yes (PRIMARY DIAGNOSTIC DRIVER)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] What is the mechanism of injury
[List Paragraph] Fall on outstretched hand (FOOSH) (Classic)
[List Paragraph] No trauma (Consider other conditions)
[List Paragraph] How did the pain begin?
[List Paragraph] Sudden (Injury)
[List Paragraph] Gradual (Not sprain)
[List Paragraph] Pain type
[List Paragraph] Sharp initially, then aching
[List Paragraph] Burning/tingling (Neurological)
[List Paragraph] Is swelling present?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Acute injury)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is the pain aggravated by movement?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Ligament involvement)
[List Paragraph] No
Objective / Observation
[List Paragraph] Is there swelling/bruising?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Acute sprain)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Does the patient experience pain with ligament stress?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Confirms sprain)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is ROM reduced?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Protective limitation)
[List Paragraph] No
Red Flag
[List Paragraph] Is there severe pain with deformity?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Fracture/dislocation)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is the patient unable to move the wrist?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Serious injury)
[List Paragraph] No
SPECIAL CONFIRMATORY TESTS
1. Ligament Stress Tests
a. Examiner applies stress to specific ligaments.
b. Sign is positive if pain/laxity occurs.
If positive, indicates ligament injury.
TFCC INJURY (Triangular Fibrocartilage Complex)
[List Paragraph] Age
[List Paragraph] 20–50 years (Common group)
[List Paragraph] Older (Degenerative tears possible)
[List Paragraph] Where is the pain located ?
[List Paragraph] Ulnar side of wrist (KEY SIGN)
[List Paragraph] Radial side (Other pathology)
[List Paragraph] Is there any history of trauma?
[List Paragraph] Yes (FOOSH or twisting)
[List Paragraph] No (Degenerative)
[List Paragraph] What aggravates the pain?
[List Paragraph] Ulnar deviation or rotation (CLASSIC)
[List Paragraph] Grip only (Less specific)
[List Paragraph] Do you experience pain when clicking or catching?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Mechanical → TFCC)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there weak grip strength?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Common finding)
[List Paragraph] No
Objective / Observation
[List Paragraph] Is there tenderness over ulnar wrist?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Primary finding)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there pain with pronation/supination?
[List Paragraph] Yes (TFCC involvement)
[List Paragraph] No
Red Flag
Severe instability?
a. Yes (Serious ligament injury)
b. No
SPECIAL CONFIRMATORY TESTS
[List Paragraph] TFCC Load Test
[List Paragraph] Wrist in ulnar deviation + axial load + rotation
[List Paragraph] Positive if pain/clicking occurs
[List Paragraph] If positive, indicates TFCC injury.
[List Paragraph] Press Test
[List Paragraph] Patient pushes up from chair using hands
[List Paragraph] Positive if ulnar wrist pain
[List Paragraph] If positive, suggests TFCC tear.
GANGLION CYST
1.         Age
[List Paragraph] 15–40 years (Common group)
[List Paragraph] Any age
2.	Where is the pain located?
[List Paragraph] Dorsal wrist (Most common site)
[List Paragraph] Volar wrist (Also possible)
3.	Do you notice a swelling/lump?
[List Paragraph] Yes (DEFINING FEATURE → ganglion cyst)
[List Paragraph] No
4.	How did the swelling begin?
[List Paragraph] Gradual (Typical)
[List Paragraph] Sudden (Less common)
5.	Pain type
[List Paragraph] Mild ache or painless (Common)
[List Paragraph] Severe pain (Consider other pathology)
6.	Does the size of the lump change?
Yes (Characteristic of ganglion cyst)
No
7.	What aggravates the pain ?
Wrist movement or loading (Supports cyst)
No change
8.	Is there any history of trauma?
Yes (May contribute)
No
Objective / Observation
[List Paragraph] Is there visible or palpable cystic swelling?
[List Paragraph] Yes (PRIMARY SIGN)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is transillumination positive?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Fluid-filled → ganglion)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there tenderness over cyst?
[List Paragraph] Yes
[List Paragraph] No
Red Flag
[List Paragraph] Hard, immobile mass?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Possible tumor → refer)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Rapid unexplained growth?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Investigate)
[List Paragraph] No
SPECIAL CONFIRMATORY TESTS
Transillumination Test
a. Light is applied through the swelling
b. Positive if light passes through
If positive, indicates fluid-filled cyst (ganglion).
INTERSECTION SYNDROME
[List Paragraph] Age
[List Paragraph] 20–50 years (Active individuals)
[List Paragraph] Any age
[List Paragraph] Where is the pain located?
[List Paragraph] Dorsal radial forearm (4–6 cm proximal to wrist) (KEY SIGN)
[List Paragraph] Radial styloid (Suggests De Quervain’s)
[List Paragraph] How did the pain begin?
[List Paragraph] Gradual (Overuse)
[List Paragraph] Sudden (Less common)
[List Paragraph] What aggravates the pain?
[List Paragraph] Repetitive wrist extension (Rowing, lifting) (CLASSIC)
[List Paragraph] Thumb movement only (De Quervain’s)
[List Paragraph] Do you feel creaking or squeaking?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Characteristic crepitus → intersection syndrome)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there swelling?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Inflammatory)
[List Paragraph] No
Objective / Observation
[List Paragraph] Is there tenderness proximal to radial styloid?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Differentiates from De Quervain’s)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there crepitus with wrist movement?
[List Paragraph] Yes (KEY FINDING)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there pain with resisted wrist extension?
[List Paragraph] Yes
[List Paragraph] No
Red Flag
History of trauma?
a. Yes (Rule out fracture)
b. No
SPECIAL CONFIRMATORY TESTS
Resisted Wrist Extension Test
a. Patient extends wrist against resistance
b. Positive if pain/crepitus occurs
If positive, indicates intersection syndrome.

KIENBÖCK’S DISEASE (AVN OF LUNATE)
[List Paragraph] Age
[List Paragraph] 20–40 years (Common group)
[List Paragraph] >50 years (Less common)
[List Paragraph] Where is the pain located?
[List Paragraph] Dorsal central wrist (KEY SIGN)
[List Paragraph] Radial/ulnar wrist (Other pathology)
[List Paragraph] How did the pain begin?
[List Paragraph] Gradual (AVN pattern)
[List Paragraph] Sudden (Consider trauma)
[List Paragraph] How will you describe the pain?
[List Paragraph] Deep, aching (AVN)
[List Paragraph] Sharp (Other pathology)
[List Paragraph] What aggravates the pain
[List Paragraph] Wrist loading/activity
[List Paragraph] Rest
[List Paragraph] Do you experience pain at rest or night?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Red flag → AVN)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is there any history of repetitive trauma or loading?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Risk factor)
[List Paragraph] No
Objective / Observation
[List Paragraph] Is there tenderness over lunate (central dorsal wrist)?
[List Paragraph] Yes (PRIMARY FINDING)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is the wrist ROM reduced?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Progressive stiffness)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Is the grip strength weak?
[List Paragraph] Yes
[List Paragraph] No
Red Flag
[List Paragraph] Do you experience progressive stiffness and pain?
[List Paragraph] Yes (AVN progression)
[List Paragraph] No
[List Paragraph] Do you experience night pain not relieved by rest?
[List Paragraph] Yes (Serious pathology)
[List Paragraph] No
SPECIAL CONFIRMATORY TESTS
No single definitive clinical test
Axial Load Test
a. Apply compression through wrist
b. Positive if central wrist pain
If positive, suggests intra-articular pathology (Kienböck’s possible)
